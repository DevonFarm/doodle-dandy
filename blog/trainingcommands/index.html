🎓 Aussiedoodle & Bernedoodle Training: 5 Essential Commands Every Puppy Should Know

Published January 15, 2025 • By Doodle Dandy Team • 8 min read
Aussiedoodles & Bernedoodles are incredibly intelligent and eager to please, making them fantastic students! Their combination of Poodle intelligence with the loyal, work-oriented nature of their other parent breeds means they not only learn quickly but actually enjoy the mental stimulation that training provides. This guide will teach you the five fundamental commands that will set your Doodle puppy up for a lifetime of success.

Why These 5 Commands Matter for Doodles

As professional Doodle breeders, we've had the privilege of watching many puppies grow into well-behaved adults. The difference between a well-trained Doodle and one that struggles often comes down to mastering these five essential commands early. These aren't just tricks—they're communication tools that will strengthen your bond and keep your intelligent Doodle mentally satisfied.

🗓️ Recommended Training Schedule

Start at 8-10 weeks old • Practice 2-3 times daily • Keep sessions short (5-10 minutes)

Consistency beats duration every time with these smart pups!

1 SIT Easy

Why it's essential: "Sit" is the foundation command that teaches impulse control and focus. For energetic Doodles, this becomes crucial for calm greetings, meal times, and before exciting activities like walks.

Step-by-Step Training:

  1. Get your puppy's attention with a high-value treat held close to their nose
  2. Slowly lift the treat up and back over their head—their bottom should naturally touch the ground
  3. The moment they sit, say "Sit!" clearly and give the treat immediately
  4. Add enthusiastic praise: "Good sit! Yes!"
  5. Practice 5-10 times per session, always ending on a successful note
Doodle Pro Tip: Aussiedoodles and Bernedoodles are food motivated and people-pleasers. Use both treats AND enthusiastic verbal praise—they love making you happy as much as getting the reward!
2 STAY Medium

Why it's essential: "Stay" teaches patience and self-control—crucial for Doodles who can be enthusiastic greeters! This command prevents door bolting, jumping on guests, and helps during grooming sessions.

Step-by-Step Training:

  1. Start with your puppy in "sit" position right in front of you
  2. Hold your hand up in a "stop" gesture and say "Stay"
  3. Take ONE small step back, wait 2 seconds, then step forward and reward
  4. Gradually increase distance and duration over multiple training sessions
  5. Always return to your puppy to release them—don't call them to break the stay
  6. Use a release word like "Okay!" or "Free!" to end the stay
Doodle Pro Tip: Doodles are eager to please, so they might break the stay to come closer to you. If this happens, simply reset and try a shorter distance. Never punish a broken stay—just start over!
3 COME Advanced

Why it's essential: A reliable recall can literally save your Doodle's life. This command is especially important for intelligent breeds who might get distracted by interesting sights, sounds, or smells during off-leash time.

Step-by-Step Training:

  1. Start indoors in a quiet space with minimal distractions
  2. Get down to your puppy's level and use an excited, happy voice
  3. Say "Come!" once and pat your legs or clap hands enthusiastically
  4. When they come to you, reward immediately with treats and praise
  5. Practice in gradually more distracting environments as they improve
  6. NEVER call your puppy to come for something unpleasant (baths, nail trims, etc.)
Doodle Pro Tip: Make coming to you the BEST thing that can happen! Use extra special treats, play sessions, or whatever your individual Doodle loves most. The recall should always be positive.
4 DOWN Medium

Why it's essential: "Down" is the ultimate calm command. It's perfect for settling your Doodle during family meals, vet visits, or when guests come over. It also helps with the Doodle tendency to be "velcro dogs" who want to be involved in everything!

Step-by-Step Training:

  1. Start with your puppy in "sit" position
  2. Hold a treat at their nose, then slowly lower it to the ground between their paws
  3. As they follow the treat down, their elbows should touch the ground
  4. The moment they're fully down, say "Down!" and reward immediately
  5. If they pop back up, that's normal—just try again with a slower lure
  6. Gradually add duration before giving the treat
Doodle Pro Tip: Some Doodles are hesitant about the down position because it feels vulnerable. Practice on soft surfaces initially and never force them down with pressure. Let the treat do the work!
5 HEEL Advanced

Why it's essential: Doodles are athletic and can be strong pullers! "Heel" teaches them to walk politely beside you, making walks enjoyable rather than a wrestling match. This is especially important as your Doodle grows to their adult size.

Step-by-Step Training:

  1. Start indoors without a leash to avoid leash pressure confusion
  2. Hold treats at your side where you want your puppy to walk
  3. Take 3-4 steps forward while encouraging them to follow the treats
  4. When they're in the right position, say "Heel!" and reward
  5. Gradually increase the number of steps before rewarding
  6. Add the leash only after they understand the concept
  7. Practice direction changes and stopping to keep them engaged
Doodle Pro Tip: Doodles are curious and love to explore! Make yourself more interesting than the environment by varying your pace, changing directions, and keeping training sessions fun and unpredictable.

Training Schedule for Success

Week 1-2: Focus on "Sit" and basic attention
Week 3-4: Add "Stay" with short durations
Week 5-6: Introduce "Down" and improve "Come"
Week 7-8: Begin "Heel" training and combine commands
Ongoing: Practice all commands in new environments

Common Doodle Training Challenges

🧠 "My Doodle Gets Distracted Easily"

This is normal for intelligent breeds! Start training in quiet environments and gradually add distractions. Keep sessions short and end on a positive note.

🎾 "My Doodle Is Too Excited to Focus"

Let them burn some energy with play before training sessions. A tired Doodle is often a more focused Doodle!

🍖 "My Doodle Isn't Food Motivated"

Try different treats, or use play and praise as rewards. Some Doodles prefer toys or attention over food.
